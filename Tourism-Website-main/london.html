London

London city is a memoir of cobbled streets and hidden gems, and you’re bound to unravel a new side of it every time you’re over here. It is located in the Southeastern area of Great Britain and has something for everyone, from history and culture to fine food and good times. London is packed with conventional tourist spots, and the best way to explore the city is to discover the lesser-known nooks and crannies. The capital is known for its world-class cuisines, fashion statement, and superb shopping.
There are various art galleries here that have preserved the rich culture and history of this city. From museums to aquariums to giant shopping centers to world-class hotels and restaurants

Places to visit

Big Ben

London’s iconic clock tower, Big Ben is one of the city’s renowned landmarks known for its gigantic bell and accuracy. The bell alone weighs 13.7 metric tons, usually associated with the entire clock tower of the Houses of Parliament

Buckingham Palace

One of the most iconic Royal buildings in the United Kingdom, Buckingham Palace is the royal residential hub of the Queen of England.The Royal Palace homes to the Throne Room which forms a central attraction for all the visitors.

London Eye

Located on London’s Southbank, London Eye is the world’s tallest cantilever wheel and an the iconic figure in the London sky. This wheel attracts nearly 4 million visitors every year.

Tower Bridge

Tower Bridge is an iconic bridge located on River Thames that has become symbolic to London.It operates through a computer control system and can be lowered down and raised remotely.

Hyde Park

Hyde Park is one of the world's greatest and phenomenal royal parks in Central London, the historical capital city of the United Kingdom.You can witness many amazing buildings, monuments, memorials, fountains, statues and a bird sanctuary inside the park.

St.Paul’s Cathedral

The Cathedral is also regarded as the Mother Church of the Diocese of London. It resides on Ludgate Hill and is deemed as one of the largest cathedrals in Europe.It’s rich historical background and grand design makes it one of the most impressive landmarks of London.
